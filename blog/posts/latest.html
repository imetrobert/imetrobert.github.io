Canadian Spotlight

What's Happening in Canada

  • 🍁May 29: Royal Bank of Canada — Expanding its Vancouver tech hub, including the Borealis AI Hub, to integrate AI across cloud, cyber, digital, and analytics. This signals a major push for AI integration in Canadian finance.
  • 🍁May 25: Government of Canada — Announced nearly $16.5 million in funding for 13 GTA businesses and organizations to accelerate AI adoption and bring new technologies to market. This injection of capital supports Canadian AI innovation and commercialization.
  • 🍁May 12: Government of Canada — Announced $66 million in funding for 44 projects through the AI Compute Access Fund to help Canadian SMEs access critical compute power. This supports the development and scaling of AI products within Canada.
  • 🍁April 30: KPMG Canada — Launched four new AI Labs across Canada (Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, with Montreal to follow) to support agentic AI development. This initiative aims to accelerate AI solution prototyping and testing for Canadian businesses.

What This Means for Canadian Business

Financial services firms like RBC are doubling down on AI infrastructure, expanding hubs and integrating AI across operations. This signals a strategic imperative to derive significant enterprise value from AI, aiming for hundreds of millions in gains. Canadian banks must ensure their AI strategies align with these aggressive growth targets, focusing on data governance and talent development to support these ambitions.

In manufacturing and retail, the BDC's $500 million LIFT initiative is a crucial step for SMEs. Only 30% of Canadian SMEs currently use AI, yet they are 24% more productive. This program directly addresses the cost and skills barriers preventing wider adoption, creating an opportunity for these businesses to close the productivity gap.

Bill C-27 (AIDA) is still working its way through Parliament, and Quebec's Law 25 privacy requirements are in full effect. Companies must ensure their AI deployments are compliant with evolving data privacy regulations. Proactive audits of AI data handling and consent mechanisms are essential before any new regulatory deadlines.

Strategic Actions for This Month

1
Audit AI data governance frameworks. Ensure compliance with Quebec Law 25 and upcoming federal regulations. Assign the Legal team and Chief Data Officer to complete this by June 30.
2

4
Review AI talent acquisition and development. Address the "talent paradox" by focusing on upskilling existing staff and recruiting specialized AI engineers. Assign the CHRO to present a revised strategy by end of Q2.
5
Commission a study on AI agent impact. Understand how autonomous software agents will affect customer interactions and internal workflows, referencing BMO's insights. Mandate the Chief Strategy Officer to deliver findings by August 15.
