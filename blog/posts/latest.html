Key AI Developments This Month

May 04
Loblaw Companies Limited

Partnered with Canadian firm Shakudo to manage and scale AI, machine learning, and data infrastructure. This allows Loblaw to build and run first-party AI applications, centralizing efforts for its Digital and Technology & Analytics teams.

May 12
Government of Canada

Announced $66 million in funding for 44 projects through the AI Compute Access Fund, supporting SMEs in accessing compute power for AI development. This initiative aims to foster homegrown innovation and keep AI talent and intellectual property within Canada.

May 19
PrairiesCan

Announced $6.8 million in funding for three Edmonton-based companies (Vertical City, Darkhorse Emergency, NTWIST) and the University of Alberta to advance AI innovation and commercialization. This investment supports scaling AI software, expanding exports, and building AI infrastructure.

May 20
Manulife Wealth & Asset Management

Launched three AI-powered sales and advisor enablement tools to streamline routine work and provide faster insights. These tools aim to free up advisors' time for client interaction and strategic advice, enhancing customer experience.

May 21
OpenAI

Projected rapid growth in advertising revenue, forecasting $2.5 billion for 2026 and up to $100 billion annually by 2030. This signals a significant monetization strategy for AI platforms, impacting the digital advertising market.

May 22
Borderless AI

Released the 2026 Canadian Employment Pulse Check, revealing 46% of employed Canadians feel AI has impacted their career trajectory, with younger workers most affected. This highlights the growing need for AI fluency and adaptation in the Canadian workforce.

May 27
Cohere & Mila

Announced a research collaboration to advance multilingual and multicultural AI evaluation, focusing on Quebec French cultural context. This partnership aims to develop AI systems that are culturally relevant and trusted in specific regional environments.

May 28
NVIDIA

Announced a $150 billion annual investment in Taiwan to solidify its position as a tech manufacturing hub for AI. This highlights global competition for AI infrastructure and manufacturing dominance.

May 29
RBC

Announced the expansion of its Vancouver banking and innovation hub, integrating AI capabilities across cloud, cyber, digital, and analytics. This move supports RBC's goal to generate significant enterprise value from AI-driven benefits.

What This Means for Canadian Business

Financial services firms like RBC and Manulife are accelerating their AI investments. RBC's expansion of its Vancouver hub and Manulife's launch of new AI-powered sales tools show a clear commitment to integrating AI for efficiency and customer engagement. These moves are critical for maintaining a competitive edge in a sector increasingly driven by data and intelligent automation. Canadian banks and insurers must prioritize scalable AI platforms and responsible deployment to unlock enterprise value.

Retailers such as Loblaw are demonstrating a pragmatic approach to AI adoption. Their partnership with Shakudo for AI infrastructure management and the integration of ChatGPT for grocery shopping signal a move towards operationalizing AI for tangible business outcomes. This focus on leveraging AI for customer experience and internal efficiency is essential for navigating the evolving retail landscape and meeting consumer expectations for personalized, seamless interactions.

The regulatory environment, particularly Bill C-27, demands proactive attention. As AI capabilities advance, ensuring compliance with evolving privacy and ethical guidelines is paramount. Canadian businesses must establish robust governance frameworks now to address potential risks and build trust with customers and regulators. Proactive engagement with these regulations will be key to sustainable AI adoption.

Strategic Actions for This Month

1
Audit current AI infrastructure and data governance practices. Identify gaps and areas for improvement to ensure compliance with Bill C-27 and Quebec Law 25. Assign the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and Legal team to complete this by June 30.
2
Pilot Cohere's enterprise-grade language models for specific internal use cases, focusing on data privacy and security. This will help assess their suitability for sensitive Canadian business data. Assign the Head of Data Science to initiate this by July 31.
3
Commission a review of your organization's AI talent strategy, considering the current market dynamics and competition. Focus on identifying skill gaps and developing plans for upskilling existing staff or targeted recruitment. Assign the Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO) to lead this effort within 60 days.
4
Negotiate with cloud providers and AI platform vendors to ensure data residency and compliance with Canadian regulations. Secure clear terms regarding data processing and storage, especially for sensitive information. Assign the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and Procurement team to address this by end of Q3.
5
Test Loblaw's ChatGPT-powered grocery integration or similar conversational AI tools in your customer service channels. Evaluate their effectiveness in driving transactions and improving customer engagement. Assign the VP of Marketing to conduct this by August 31.
