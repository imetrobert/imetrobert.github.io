Canada's AI strategy, focused on sovereign compute and retaining intellectual property, is set to be released next week. This signals a significant governmental push to solidify our position in the global AI market. For Canadian businesses, this means increased focus on domestic AI development and deployment. This analysis will equip you to respond effectively to these shifts.

Key AI Developments This Month

May 27
: Liaison Strategies

A survey shows 46% of Canadians used AI chatbots for medical advice, but 68% prefer waiting for a human doctor over an immediate AI diagnosis. This highlights a public appetite for AI assistance but a strong preference for human oversight in critical decisions, impacting healthcare AI deployment.

May 25
: Government of Canada

Announced nearly $16.5 million in funding for 13 Toronto-area businesses and organizations to facilitate AI adoption. This investment aims to accelerate AI technologies to market and broaden adoption across key sectors, strengthening Canada's AI ecosystem.

May 21
: Northern Signal

Reported Cohere leads Canadian AI companies with CA$2.12 billion in funding, with Toronto dominating the top 10. This underscores Toronto's strength in generative AI and the concentration of AI talent and venture capital in Canada's tech hubs.

May 19
: Government of Canada

Announced support for five Alberta-based organizations through the Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII) to improve productivity and commercialize AI solutions. This initiative aims to advance Canada's domestic AI capacity and foster economic growth in the region.

May 13
: Government of Canada

Announced $66 million in funding for 44 Canadian companies through the AI Compute Access Fund. This program, part of the Canadian Sovereign AI Compute Strategy, aims to help SMEs access critical compute power, fostering innovation and scaling AI products within Canada.

April 24
: Business Development Bank of Canada (BDC)

Launched the $500 million LIFT initiative to accelerate AI adoption by Canadian SMEs. This program offers financing and advisory services, prioritizing Canadian-developed AI tools to boost productivity and support the domestic tech ecosystem.

April 17
: Signal49 Research

Highlighted the rapid advancement of agentic AI, capable of reasoning and autonomous task execution. This development signals a shift towards AI co-workers and the need for businesses to adapt to AI's evolving capabilities.

April 15
: Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada

Launched the call for applications for the AI Sovereign Compute Infrastructure Program. This program, part of the Canadian Sovereign AI Compute Strategy, aims to build large-scale, Canadian-based AI supercomputing systems to support research and innovation.

What This Means for Canadian Business

Financial services and technology firms must prepare for an AI-driven future. BMO Financial Group's chief AI officer noted that customer agents will soon be making purchasing and investment decisions on behalf of individuals. This requires Canadian banks like RBC and TD to rethink their customer engagement strategies, focusing on how to market to these AI agents. Companies like Shopify must also consider how their platforms will interface with these emerging AI agents.

In manufacturing and healthcare, the adoption of agentic AI presents both opportunities and risks. While AI can enhance productivity and diagnostic support, as seen with Liaison Strategies' findings on AI in healthcare, Canadians still prefer human oversight for critical decisions. This means Canadian manufacturers must focus on AI applications that augment human capabilities, rather than replace them entirely, ensuring trust and safety.

The upcoming release of Canada's national AI strategy, coupled with existing regulations like Bill C-27 and Quebec's Law 25, demands immediate attention. Leaders must ensure their AI deployments are compliant and ethically sound. By June 1, 2026, organizations must submit applications for the AI Sovereign Compute Infrastructure Program, indicating a critical window for strategic alignment with national AI goals.

Strategic Actions for This Month

1
Audit current AI governance frameworks against Bill C-27 and Quebec's Law 25 requirements. Assign the Legal and Compliance teams to complete this by June 30.
2
Pilot agentic AI tools in non-critical customer service workflows to assess their efficacy and employee acceptance. The CTO should oversee this pilot, with results due by end of Q3.
3
Commission a review of your company's AI compute infrastructure needs in light of the new AI Sovereign Compute Infrastructure Program. The CIO is responsible for initiating this review within 30 days.
4
Negotiate with Canadian AI vendors for preferential rates or terms, aligning with BDC's LIFT initiative focus. The Procurement department should lead this effort, aiming for new agreements by August 31.
5
Block time for senior leadership to deeply understand the implications of AI agents on customer interaction and marketing strategies. The Chief Marketing Officer should schedule this for the next executive offsite.
