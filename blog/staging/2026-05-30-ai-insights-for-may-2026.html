What This Means for Canadian Business

The Bank of Canada's May 13 speech on AI's economic impact underscores its potential to boost productivity and growth, a crucial point for financial services firms. Canadian banks, like RBC and TD, are already navigating AI's integration, with 30% of finance and insurance firms already using AI. This trend suggests a significant operational shift is underway, demanding strategic adaptation to leverage AI for enhanced efficiency and competitive advantage.

The recent government funding for AI compute access, as seen with the AI Compute Access Fund, directly supports Canadian manufacturing and retail sectors. Companies like Loblaw are already deploying AI for agentic shopping experiences. This support enables these businesses to build and scale AI products, driving innovation and ensuring they remain competitive in an increasingly automated market.

The upcoming AI strategy and ongoing regulatory discussions, including potential updates to Bill C-27 and Quebec's Law 25, signal increasing scrutiny on AI governance. With the federal government set to release its AI strategy soon, Canadian businesses must proactively address compliance and ethical considerations. The rising adoption rates, with 12.2% of Canadian businesses using AI in 2025, highlight the urgency for robust governance frameworks.

Strategic Actions for This Month

1
Audit AI usage in financial services operations: In response to the Bank of Canada's concerns about AI amplifying financial system vulnerabilities, the CTO should conduct a comprehensive audit of all AI tools and models currently in use. This audit should assess model risk, cyber risk, and data security. Deadline: End of Q3 2026.
2
Pilot GenAI for clinical documentation support: Following the CSA Group's report on GenAI in healthcare, healthcare providers should pilot generative AI tools for clinical documentation and information retrieval. This pilot should focus on clinician burnout and system constraints. Deadline: Before June 30, 2026.
3
Develop a Canadian-centric AI model strategy: In response to Cohere's launch of Command R+ with enhanced bilingual support, the CTO should commission a review of current AI model providers. The goal is to identify opportunities to deploy Canadian-built AI solutions for French-English operations, considering data residency. Deadline: Within 60 days.
4
Assess AI compute infrastructure needs: Given the Government of Canada's AI Compute Access Fund, the CFO should assess current and future compute infrastructure requirements. This assessment should inform potential applications for government funding to support AI development and scaling. Deadline: End of Q2 2026.
5
Commission an AI governance framework review: In light of the upcoming national AI strategy and ongoing regulatory discussions, the Legal team should commission a review of the existing AI governance framework. This review should ensure compliance with evolving privacy laws and prepare for new AI-specific regulations. Deadline: Before September 30, 2026.
